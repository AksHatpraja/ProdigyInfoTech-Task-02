## Cluster 0: Older Moderate Customers

* Average age is about **55 years**.
* Medium annual income.
* Moderate spending habits.
* These customers spend carefully and make planned purchases.

**Business Strategy:**

* Offer loyalty programs and discounts.
* Promote products related to family and health needs.

---

## Cluster 1: Premium Customers

* Average age is about **33 years**.
* Very high income.
* Very high spending score.

**Characteristics:**

* Most valuable customers.
* Frequently purchase products and services.
* Likely interested in premium brands.

**Business Strategy:**

* Provide VIP memberships.
* Offer personalized recommendations.
* Focus on premium and luxury products.

---

## Cluster 2: Young High-Spending Customers

* Average age is about **26 years**.
* Lowest income among all clusters.
* High spending score.

**Characteristics:**

* Young shoppers.
* Spend a large portion of their income.
* Influenced by trends and promotions.

**Business Strategy:**

* Use social media marketing.
* Offer trendy products and special deals.
* Create student and youth-focused campaigns.

---

## Cluster 3: Average Customers

* Average age is about **27 years**.
* Medium income.
* Medium spending score.

**Characteristics:**

* Balanced purchasing behavior.
* Regular customers with normal spending patterns.

**Business Strategy:**

* Maintain engagement through offers and rewards.
* Encourage additional purchases through bundles.

---

## Cluster 4: Wealthy but Careful Customers

* Average age is about **44 years**.
* Highest income group.
* Lowest spending score.

**Characteristics:**

* Have money but spend cautiously.
* May compare products before purchasing.

**Business Strategy:**

* Offer exclusive benefits.
* Highlight product quality and long-term value.
* Use targeted marketing campaigns.

# Conclusion

The K-Means algorithm divided the 200 customers into **5 distinct groups**:

1. **Cluster 0:** Older customers with moderate spending.
2. **Cluster 1:** High-income, high-spending premium customers.
3. **Cluster 2:** Young customers with low income but high spending.
4. **Cluster 3:** Average customers with balanced spending behavior.
5. **Cluster 4:** High-income customers who spend very little.

Among all groups, **Cluster 1** is the most profitable segment because customers have both **high income and high spending behavior**, while **Cluster 4** represents an opportunity for the business to encourage more spending through targeted promotions and personalized offers.
